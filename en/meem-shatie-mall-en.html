  • About Gulf International Bank

    Open or Close

    Gulf International Bank (GIB), the majority shareholder of the Public Investment Fund of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, is one of the leading banks in the Middle East, offering a range of financial products and services that meet the specific needs of its customers, including investment banking, asset management and money market instruments. Arranging public and private placements, participating in corporate mergers, and managing the issuance of sukuk, bonds and banking in compliance with Islamic law.


    GIB is licensed by the Central Bank of Bahrain as a conventional wholesale bank and is owned by the six GCC countries. Most of its shares are owned by the Public Investment Fund of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia (97.2%). In addition to its two main companies, Gulf International Bank (UK) Limited and GIB Capital. The bank has branches in London, New York, Riyadh, Dhahran and Jeddah and has recently been granted a permit to conduct wholesale banking in Abu Dhabi. The bank also has a representative office in Dubai and a representative office in Beirut.
